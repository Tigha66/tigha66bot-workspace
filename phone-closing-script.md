### PHASE 2: DISCOVERY (1-4 minutes)

**Goal:** Make them feel the pain. Ask these questions:

```
"Quick question — how do you currently ask customers for reviews?"

[Listen. Common answers:]
→ "We don't" → "Got it. So you're leaving reviews on the table."
→ "We ask in person" → "Nice! What % of customers actually leave one?"
→ "We send an email" → "Yeah, email gets about 12% response. SMS gets 87%."
→ "We use [competitor]" → "Interesting! How's that working for you?"
```

```
"And how many new reviews did you get last month?"

[Let them answer. If they don't know:]
→ "No worries — most businesses don't track. But if you're getting less 
   than 10-15, you're probably losing customers to competitors with more."
```

```
"And when a customer doesn't leave a review, what happens?"

[They'll say "nothing" or "we don't follow up"]
→ "Exactly. And that's the problem. Happy customers forget. My tool 
   automatically follows up until they leave one — or until you tell it 
   to stop."
```

```
"Last question — if you could get 5x more reviews without any extra work, 
what would that mean for your business?"

[Let them answer. This makes THEM sell themselves.]
→ "More customers" → "Exactly. More reviews = higher Google ranking = 
   more calls."
→ "Better ranking" → "Yes! And better ranking means more inbound calls."
→ "More revenue" → "100%. One extra customer per month pays for this forever."
```

## 🎙️ VOICE TIPS

✅ **Do:**
- Speak slowly and clearly
- Smile while talking (sounds warmer)
- Pause after questions (let them think)
- Match their energy level
- Use their name 2-3 times during call

❌ **Don't:**
- Rush through the script
- Sound robotic (be conversational)
- Interrupt them
- Argue with objections
- Sound desperate ("Please buy!")
